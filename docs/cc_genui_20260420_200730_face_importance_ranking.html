Status: Working analysis — not committed to implementation

APE has two distinct architectural gaps, surfaced by two independent observations during real refinement work. First, an external review (ChatGPT) of an APE-refined prompt drew the categorical distinction between structural control (testable instructions) and probabilistic bias (moral/value framing). APE measures register engagement uniformly across both — silent on which is which, silent on which a given task needs. Second, a four-iteration field refinement showed that the BGE bridge itself routes shared vocabulary into the more abstract of two competing faces: ethics escalation from owe to culpable, forbidden, virtue, lie, sabotage, moral duty never moved ethics off 0.10 because axiology absorbed every duty-adjacent term. This is not an inhabitation failure — it is a routing collision the bridge cannot resolve with current vocabulary attribution.

The proposal addresses both: a Layer A (engine) intervention extending APE's existing disambiguation infrastructure to enforce four routing principles (specificity wins, evaluative triad ordering, foundation-before-evaluation, directional pairing) plus optional directional resonance; and a Layer B (skill) intervention that classifies the request, loads a per-task priority template, computes priority-weighted gaps, and recommends specific prompt-engineering patterns to close them. Layer A fixes can the bridge see the activation at all?; Layer B fixes which gap should the user prioritize?. Build both, but stage them: Layer B is reversible skill content; Layer A touches the build artifact and needs evidence per principle.

1. The Triggering Inputs

1a. External review (ChatGPT) — the structural-vs-bias distinction

What the reviewer said about an APE-refined prompt

  • Moral framing ("be honest," "do not fabricate") demonstrably affects model behavior in some task types — primarily open-ended reasoning, ambiguous QA, hallucination-prone generation.
  • The mechanism is not moral comprehension. It is activation of learned response patterns associated with cautious phrasing and longer reasoning chains.
  • In deterministic engineering contexts (precise edits, required invariants, schema-bound output) the limiting factor is not whether the model "tries to be honest" but whether it has unambiguous, checkable constraints.
  • Moral framing is a probabilistic bias. Structural constraints are a structural control. Both can be present in the same prompt; the strongest prompts combine them; the issue is balance.

1b. Field observations — vocabulary routing collisions

Independently, a four-iteration refinement session on an execution-prompt for an EOF generator produced a structurally different observation. APE's bridge appears to systematically misattribute shared vocabulary, and inhabitation does not fix it.

Ethics sank into axiology. Vocabulary escalated from owe, trust, warrant (v2) to culpable, forbidden, virtue, lie, sabotage, moral duty (v4). Ethics stayed at 0.10. Axiology climbed from 0.58 → 0.60. The BGE bridge appears to route duty-adjacent vocabulary into axiology rather than ethics when both could claim it.
Phenomenology activated cleanly when given distinctive vocabulary. "Three surfaces exist and are not the same surface" triggered phenomenology (0.50, new to dominant). When that paragraph shortened in v4, phenomenology dropped out of dominants. Surface/depth framing has a clear phenomenological signature.
Semiotics and hermeneutics moved in lockstep. Both rose 0.62/0.58 (v1) → 0.65/0.65 (v3). They share vocabulary territory and resist differentiation under the current bridge.
Officially-paired faces under-resonated. Ontology↔praxeology (the server's top resonance) stayed at 0.11 across all iterations despite the prompt explicitly describing entities AND ordering actions. Likely cause: execution prompts that receive a pre-designed plan read as ontology-heavy but praxeology-light, because the action-structure is inherited, not generated. The symmetric pairing has no way to express this directionality.

These four observations are not noise. They reveal a structural property of the bridge: shared vocabulary defaults to the more abstract of two competing faces, related faces that share idioms cannot be differentiated, and the symmetric pairing model cannot distinguish "this prompt grounds X in Y" from "this prompt does X and Y in parallel."

3. The Structural-Control vs Probabilistic-Bias Distinction

This is not a new idea in the prompt engineering literature — it appears under several names: hard vs soft constraints; verifiable vs unverifiable instructions; scaffolding vs steering; decoder constraints vs prior shaping. The pattern is consistent: one category produces outputs that can be machine-checked against the instruction; the other produces distributional shifts whose effect is only measurable in aggregate behavior.

Working two-axis taxonomy

AxisQuestionExamples (yes / no)
Verifiability Can a checker confirm the instruction was followed in this single output? Schema match (yes), citation present (yes) / "Be respectful" (no), "Think carefully" (no)
Compositionality Does the instruction compose with other instructions deterministically? Output format (yes), step numbering (yes) / Persona (no), value framing (no)

5. Four Routing Principles

The four field observations imply four classical orderings the bridge currently does not enforce. These are the substantive content of Layer A.

Principle 1 — Specificity wins When vocabulary activates two faces, the face with the more restrictive commitment claims it. Culpable is more specific to ethics than to axiology; ethics should win. Systematic is more specific to methodology than to heuristics; methodology should win. Currently the bridge defers to the more abstract face because abstract centroids encompass more of the vocabulary cloud.
Principle 2 — Evaluative faces are a triad with internal order Axiology (worth), Ethics (duty), and Teleology (purpose) share evaluative territory. The classical philosophical chain is teleology grounds ethics grounds axiology — what a thing is for shapes what is owed to it shapes what it is worth. When all three could claim shared vocabulary, rank by this chain so the grounding face activates first.
Principle 3 — Foundation faces are prior to evaluation faces Ontology and Epistemology are prior to axiology/ethics/teleology in almost every classical order (Kant, Aristotle, Husserl). A prompt cannot activate evaluative faces coherently without foundational commitments. If an evaluative face dominates while its foundation face is below threshold, the measurement should flag that as an incoherent stance, not a stylistic choice. This is a new failure mode the engine could surface.
Principle 4 — Paired faces need direction, not symmetry The six resonance pairs are currently symmetric (ontology↔praxeology, etc.). But being-grounds-doing, knowing-grounds-proceeding, encoding-grounds-interpreting are directional. A directional resonance model would let the grounding face activate first and the grounded face inherit energy — preventing the symmetric weak-resonance failure observed when the prompt addressed entities-then-actions but ontology↔praxeology stayed at 0.11.

7. The Architectural Gap (Output Side)

Beyond routing, APE's interpretation layer is also incomplete. It does not measure:

  1. Control type composition. What fraction of activated weight is structural vs bias?
  2. Task fit. Whether the activated register matches what the task requires.
  3. Intervention effectiveness. Whether known prompt-engineering patterns appropriate to the task are present.

The skill currently asks the user to make all three judgments unaided. That is workable for experienced users but provides no path to systematic improvement: the same neglected face is reported the same way regardless of whether the task is engineering or creative.

10. Proposed Architecture — Two Layers

Layer A Engine: corrected vocabulary routing + foundation/evaluation precedence

Lives in the build-time semantic-bridge artifact and (for directional resonance) the engine's tension/gem/harmonization computation.

A1. Disambiguation entries enforcing Principles 1–3

Extend scripts/build_semantic_bridge.py and the resulting _disambig_meta entries to encode:

  • Specificity overrides: when a token has restrictive use in face A and broad use in face B, route to A. Seed list from the vocabulary routing collision table above.
  • Evaluative-triad ordering: when a token is shared across teleology / ethics / axiology, attribute by the chain teleology > ethics > axiology unless context-words tip otherwise.
  • Foundation-precedence flag: a new output field per measurement noting when an evaluative face is dominant while its foundation face is below threshold. The engine reports this; the skill consumes it as an "incoherent stance" warning.

A2. Directional resonance (Principle 4)

Modify the harmonization and gem computations so each cube pair has a designated grounding direction (e.g., ontology→praxeology, epistemology→methodology, semiotics→hermeneutics). Resonance becomes a directed metric: high when the grounding face activates first and the grounded face inherits, low when both activate independently without grounding flow. This is a real engine change — bumps version to 0.8.0 minimum.

A3. Optional: control-type classification

Add a control_type field per face classifying activations as structural, bias, or mixed (per Section 4). Engine reports both raw activation and structural/bias share. Smaller change than A2 but conceptually tied to Layer B's task-priority framework.

Layer B Skill: task classification + priority gap analysis + intervention recommendation

Lives entirely in skills/prompt-refiner/SKILL.md. No engine dependency.

Step 2.5: Classify task type

Inferred from the prompt or asked. Output: a task-type identifier, optionally a hybrid combination.

Step 2.6: Compute priority gaps

for each face:
    priority   = template[task_type][face]   # H = 1.0, M = 0.5, L = 0.0
    activation = basis.coordinate[face].weight
    gap        = priority - normalized(activation)
    record (face, priority, activation, gap)

sort by gap descending

Output as three lists:

  • Critical gaps — priority H, activation low — must address
  • Acceptable neglect — priority L, activation low — confirm not addressing is correct
  • Diffusion candidates — priority L, activation high — candidates for compression

Step 2.6b: Routing-failure suspicion

Before recommending intervention, check whether the gap is plausibly a Layer A routing collision. If the user has clearly inhabited a face's vocabulary (skill prompts the user to confirm) but the face stays at floor, flag the gap as likely routing collision rather than inhabitation gap. This would prevent the four-iteration trap where the user keeps escalating ethics vocabulary that gets routed to axiology.

Step 2.7: Suggest interventions from pattern library

For each critical gap (and not flagged as routing collision), look up patterns that primarily activate that face and recommend the lightest-touch one not already present.

Example output

Critical gap: Methodology (priority H, activation 0.12). Your task is engineering work. Consider: Chain of Thought scaffold, worked-example demonstration, or Plan-and-Execute structure. Lowest-cost intervention: add an explicit "Reasoning:" / "Result:" output template.

Routing-collision suspected: Ethics (priority H, activation 0.10). Vocabulary scan finds culpable, forbidden, virtue in your prompt. Engine likely routing these to axiology (currently 0.60). Recommend: file a disambiguation request rather than rewriting; or add explicit ethical-framing structure (rule list, deontic verbs) that the engine's existing semiotic+ethics co-pattern can detect.

Step 2.8: Re-measure with priority overlay

After the user rewrites, re-measure and compare gaps against priorities. Plateau condition: critical gaps closed, acceptable neglect confirmed, diffusion candidates compressed, no foundation-precedence flag.

11. Evidence Requirements

The proposal rests on six empirical claims, each testable inside the project's existing benchmark infrastructure with modest extension.

Layer A claims

  1. Specificity routing improves attribution. After adding disambiguation entries from the routing collision table, ethics-heavy prompts (v3 and v4 from the field observation session) should show ethics activation rising from ~0.10 toward the 0.40–0.50 band, with axiology dropping from 0.60 toward 0.30–0.40. Test: rerun the existing four-iteration prompts before and after disambiguation entries; confirm directional shift.
  2. Foundation-precedence flag fires correctly. Prompts with high ethics/axiology and low ontology/epistemology should be flagged. Test: hand-craft 5 such prompts; verify flag fires; verify flag does not fire on balanced prompts.
  3. Directional resonance recovers grounded prompts. The execution-prompt that scored 0.11 on ontology↔praxeology should score higher on the directional ontology→praxeology metric (energy flowing from grounded entities to inherited actions). Test: rerun the same prompt under directional resonance; verify the directional score is not symmetric.

Layer B claims

  1. Face control-type classification is accurate. Faces called "structural" should produce verifiable outputs when activated. Test: hand-craft prompts that activate one face strongly; measure whether outputs admit machine-checkable invariants.
  2. Task-type priority table is correct. Tasks classified as "engineering" should improve when high-priority faces are strengthened, not when low-priority faces are. Test: take a benchmark of engineering prompts; vary face activation; measure task success.
  3. Pattern-to-face mapping is correct. Adding CoT to a prompt should shift methodology activation upward, not phenomenology. Test: measure face activation before and after applying each pattern.

Composite test from field observations

Rerun v3 of the four-iteration prompt under both Layer A (corrected routing) and Layer B (priority-aware skill). Expected:

  • Ethics rises from 0.10 toward 0.40–0.50 (Layer A: dense moral vocabulary now correctly attributed)
  • Axiology drops from 0.60 toward 0.30–0.40 (Layer A: was over-claiming)
  • Coherence rises from 0.36 toward 0.50+ (faces now cross-pollinate correctly)
  • Strongest resonance shifts from ontology↔praxeology (symmetric weak) to a directional pair (ethics→axiology, epistemology→hermeneutics) — Layer A directional resonance only
  • Skill output flags ethics as a closed critical gap rather than an open one (Layer B)

If none shift, the issue is BGE compression, not vocabulary routing — and the framework needs to operate at the face-weight normalization layer rather than the vocabulary attribution layer.

Anti-pattern: "smart" task classification by an LLM call inside the engine

Tempting but wrong. The engine should remain stateless and computationally cheap (numpy-only at runtime per ADR-005). Task classification belongs in the skill (where the LLM is already engaged with the user's prompt). Putting it in the engine couples the engine to a model dependency it currently does not have.

13. Risks and Open Questions

Risks

  1. Over-correcting routing. Aggressive specificity overrides could create new collisions where ethics now over-claims worth-bearing vocabulary. Mitigation: evidence-driven additions; rerun the four-iteration prompts after each batch.
  2. Codifying contested theory. The pattern library cites results that are not all robust; the evaluative-triad ordering (teleology grounds ethics grounds axiology) is one philosophical lineage among several. Mitigation: source-cite each entry; treat the table and the principles as working hypotheses.
  3. Task-type rigidity. Real prompts mix types. The new "execution prompt (inheriting plan)" category came directly from a real prompt; others will too. Mitigation: support hybrid types; let users override.
  4. Skill complexity. The skill is already long. Adding 5 steps and 2 tables risks information overload. Mitigation: tight tabular presentation, progressive disclosure.
  5. Anchoring effect. Once users see a priority ranking, they may defer to it and stop thinking. Mitigation: phrase priorities as defaults to be overridden.
  6. Co-evolution with the engine. If the skill embeds priority data that should drive engine evolution, divergence becomes a maintenance burden. Mitigation: version-pin the skill to the engine version it was tested against.
  7. Directional resonance breaks symmetric tests. v4 changes the harmonization output shape; existing tests in tests/test_math/test_harmonization.py assume symmetric scores. Mitigation: dual-output (symmetric and directional) during transition.

Open Questions

  1. Is the BGE compression itself the limit? If Layer A disambiguation does not move ethics, the bottleneck is the BGE embedding's inability to separate ethics-vocabulary from axiology-vocabulary at the centroid level. That would require revisiting the embedding source — out of scope for this proposal but worth flagging.
  2. Should the foundation/evaluation hierarchy be a separate measurement, not a flag? A "philosophical-stance coherence" score that explicitly penalizes evaluative-without-foundational activation would be richer than a binary flag.
  3. Can directional resonance be expressed without changing the cube-pair model? The current ADR-002 specifies symmetric pairing as part of the cube model. Directional resonance is consistent with the cube geometry (each pair has an inside/outside surface), so it may be a refinement rather than a contradiction.
  4. What's the right default when the user can't or won't classify? Probably: assume "general" with a flat priority profile and report uniformly (current behavior).
  5. How does the framework handle prompts that legitimately want a low-priority face? A creative prompt that wants strong methodology (sonnet structure) needs override capability. Expose priority overrides as first-class user input.
  6. Are the four routing principles compatible? Specificity (P1) and evaluative-triad (P2) could conflict on a token like justify — specificity says ethics if the prompt is deontic, but the triad says teleology grounds ethics. The composition rule needs to be defined: contextual specificity overrides triad ordering when both apply.

14. Recommendation

Build v1 immediately; fund v2 with empirical evidence; defer v3–v5

v1 (skill-only) is reversible and cheap. It provides task-aware interpretation and routing-collision suspicion immediately. The pattern-library and priority-table content is the substantive work; the skill integration is mechanical.

v2 (Layer A disambiguation entries) is the highest-leverage next move because it directly addresses the field observation that no amount of inhabitation moves ethics off 0.10. v2 should be funded by running the composite test from Section 11 and iterating on the routing collision table until ethics-heavy prompts attribute correctly. v2 is empirical work, not theoretical.

v3–v5 require evidence from v1 and v2 to justify. Foundation-precedence reporting (v3) is the smallest engine change; directional resonance (v4) is the largest and should be deferred until v3 demonstrates that the framework holds.

The most important conceptual move is acknowledging that APE has two independent gaps, not one. The external review surfaced the output-side gap (register vs control); the field observations surfaced the input-side gap (vocabulary routing). Both are real; both have separate fixes; neither subsumes the other. A unified recommendation that addressed only one would miss half the actual problem.
