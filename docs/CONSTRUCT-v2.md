# Construct Specification v2: Philosophical Geometry

## 1. Purpose

The Construct is a formal interpretive and organizational framework for representing structured possibility, observation, inter-domain interaction, and system-wide synthesis. It defines a fixed architecture in which domains of meaning are internally organized as grids and externally related through mediated loci of interaction. The Construct is intended to support analysis, comparison, harmonization, and recursive reuse of structured knowledge.

This specification supersedes v1. It revises the Construct from a 10-plane Tetractys-organized system to a 12-face Vector Equilibrium-organized system. The revision preserves all structural principles from v1 while expanding the geometric foundation.

---

## 2. Ontological Status

The Construct is not merely a diagram. It is a semantic system with a geometry, a topology, and a set of roles for its internal elements.

At minimum, the Construct may be treated as all of the following at once:

* a formal conceptual model
* a multidomain interpretive framework
* a lattice-graph hybrid
* a positional schema for structured possibility
* a mediation model for cross-domain interaction
* a recursive scaffold for higher-order composition
* a 2D-in-3D skeletal frame awaiting dimensional extrusion

The Construct is therefore both representational and operational. It represents a structured world of possibility, and it provides rules for how that world may be traversed, interpreted, compared, and integrated.

---

## 3. Core Commitments

The Construct rests on the following fixed commitments.

1. There are exactly 12 faces.
2. Each face contains exactly 144 points.
3. Each face is organized as a 12 × 12 grid.
4. Each point is pre-positioned and addressable by coordinate.
5. Position is semantically meaningful.
6. Edge points are more potent than center points.
7. Opposed edge positions define structural spectrums.
8. Faces are connected to one another through nexi.
9. A central gem unifies the inter-face system.
10. The same structure may be viewed both as a network and as a wheel.
11. The Construct supports recursive embedding.
12. The x-axis carries invariant meta-meaning across all faces: constitutive character (what kind of thing this is within the domain).
13. The y-axis carries invariant meta-meaning across all faces: dispositional orientation (how the thing moves, relates, or engages within the domain).
14. The inter-face architecture (nexi, gems, spokes, wheel, central gem) is generatively deduced from the face-level assignments, not independently defined.
15. The organizing geometry is the Vector Equilibrium (cuboctahedron), with the Construct's outer shell conforming to the rhombic dodecahedron (the dual).
16. The 12 faces are organized as 6 complementary pairs, each pair sharing a cube face with one domain facing inward (theoretical/comprehension) and one facing outward (applied/action).
17. Inter-face relationships are mediated by positional correspondence (shared coordinate system via polarity convention) rather than point-to-point cross-face edges.
18. Harmonization between paired faces arises from their shared surface and aligned coordinates — they are structurally tuned to receive each other's activations.

## 4. Organizing Geometry

### 4.1 The Vector Equilibrium (inner skeleton)

The cuboctahedron — Buckminster Fuller's Vector Equilibrium — provides the inter-face connection structure. It is the only polyhedron where every vertex is equidistant from the center and from its neighbors.

| Element | Count | Maps to |
|---|---|---|
| Vertices | 12 | 12 face positions (domain locations in 3D space) |
| Edges | 24 | 24 sub-dimensional extremes (12 faces × 2 axes) |
| Triangular faces | 8 | 8 triads of mutually connected domains |
| Square faces | 6 | 6 quartets of domains forming cycles |
| Center | 1 | Central gem |

Properties:
* Every vertex equidistant from center — no face is privileged
* Each vertex has exactly 4 neighbors — each face has 4 primary connections
* Each face's 4 sub-dimensional extremes map to its 4 cuboctahedral edges

### 4.2 The Rhombic Dodecahedron (outer shell)

The dual of the cuboctahedron. Each vertex of the cuboctahedron corresponds to a face of the rhombic dodecahedron.

| Element | Count | Maps to |
|---|---|---|
| Faces | 12 | 12 domains — each domain IS a face |
| Vertices | 14 | 8 triad-points + 6 quartet-points |
| Edges | 24 | 24 sub-dimensional extremes (shared with inner skeleton) |

Properties:
* Each face is a rhombus — 4 edges, 4 corners
* The rhombic dodecahedron is space-filling — it tiles 3D with no gaps
* Space-filling property provides the geometric basis for recursive embedding (Commitment 11)

### 4.3 The Cube Model (complementary pairing)

The 12 domains are organized as 6 complementary pairs. Each pair occupies one face of a cube, with one domain facing inward (theoretical/comprehension) and one facing outward (applied/action).

| Cube Face | Inward (theoretical) | Outward (applied) | Shared concern |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | Ontology | Praxeology | Being ↔ Doing |
| 2 | Epistemology | Methodology | Knowing ↔ Proceeding |
| 3 | Axiology | Ethics | Valuing ↔ Judging |
| 4 | Teleology | Heuristics | Purpose ↔ Strategy |
| 5 | Phenomenology | Aesthetics | Experiencing ↔ Recognizing form |
| 6 | Semiotics | Hermeneutics | Encoding ↔ Decoding |

Properties:
* Each pair is a theoretical-practical reflection of the same concern
* The inward face understands; the outward face acts
* Paired faces share a surface — the same coordinate system, the same polarity convention, the same structural archetype at each position
* Harmonization arises from this shared surface: an activation at position (x,y) on one face has a natural correspondent at (x,y) on its paired face

### 4.4 Nexus stratification

The cube model stratifies the 66 nexi into three kinds based on geometric relationship:

| Kind | Count | Geometric relationship | Character |
|---|---|---|---|
| Paired | 6 | Inside ↔ outside of same cube face (6 faces × 1 pair) | Complementary reflection — theoretical and practical aspects of the same concern |
| Adjacent | 48 | Domains on different cube faces that share an edge (12 edges × 4 domain-pairs) | Proximal relationship — neighboring concerns |
| Opposite | 12 | Domains on opposite cube faces (3 opposite face-pairs × 4 domain-pairs) | Distal relationship — distant concerns |

Per domain: 1 paired + 8 adjacent + 2 opposite = 11 nexi.

The paired nexi are structurally special: they connect domains that are tuned to each other through a shared surface. The gem produced by a paired nexus carries complementary meaning — not tension between strangers, but resonance between counterparts.

### 4.5 Relationship between geometries

Three geometric models describe different aspects of the same structure:

| Geometry | Role | What it describes |
|---|---|---|
| Cube (6 faces × inside/outside) | Pairing and harmonization | Which domains are complementary counterparts |
| Cuboctahedron (12 vertices) | Equipoise and connection | Equal structural weight, latent 3D positioning |
| Rhombic dodecahedron (12 faces) | Outer shell and tiling | Recursive embedding via space-filling |

These are not competing models. The cube provides pairing. The cuboctahedron provides the connection lattice (emergent from equal weighting — latent, not imposed). The rhombic dodecahedron provides the outer form. All three describe the same 12-domain system from different perspectives.

The cuboctahedron is latent: it is the shape the system would assume in 3D space given equal inter-face weighting. It does not need to be computed in stage 1 or 2. It becomes relevant when the Construct is positioned in 3D space (stage 3).

### 4.6 Dimensional staging

The Construct is built in stages:

1. **2D**: Each face is a flat 12×12 grid with full internal structure (outer lines, inner lines, corners, spectrums, points)
2. **2D-in-3D**: The 12 flat faces are positioned and oriented in 3D space according to the Vector Equilibrium geometry. The faces remain flat — they have position and angle but no depth. The skeletal connections (nexi) run between faces through 3D space.
3. **3D** (future): The flat faces are extruded into volumetric meaning. This stage requires the skeleton to have dimensional integrity first.

This specification defines stages 1 and 2. Stage 3 is deferred.

## 5. Face Structure (Intra-face Geometry)

### 5.1 Grid dimensions

Each face is a 12 × 12 Cartesian grid.

* Width: 12
* Height: 12
* Total points: 144
* Coordinates: integer pairs from (0,0) through (11,11)

### 5.2 Axis meta-meaning

The two axes carry invariant meaning across all 12 faces.

**X-axis: Constitutive Character**
What kind of thing this is within the domain. The nature, mode, or structural identity of the phenomenon. At x=0, the most elemental or foundational form. At x=11, the most comprehensive or expansive form.

**Y-axis: Dispositional Orientation**
How the thing moves, relates, or engages within the domain. The tendency, trajectory, or dynamic posture of the phenomenon. At y=0, the most stable or grounded tendency. At y=11, the most fluid or exploratory tendency.

Each face instantiates these meta-axes with domain-specific sub-dimensions (see §7).

### 5.3 Point classification

Every point is classified by its position on the grid.

**Edge points**: Points where at least one coordinate is 0 or 11. These sit on the outer lines of the face.
* Count: 44 per face (12 + 12 + 10 + 10)
* Total across Construct: 528

**Center points**: Points where both coordinates are in the range 1–10. These sit on the inner lines of the face.
* Count: 100 per face
* Total across Construct: 1200

**Corners**: Points where both coordinates are extremes: (0,0), (11,0), (0,11), (11,11).
* Count: 4 per face
* Total across Construct: 48

**Edge midpoints**: The two central positions on each edge. On a 12-point edge (0–11), positions 5 and 6 are the midpoints.
* Count: 8 per face (2 per edge × 4 edges)
* Total across Construct: 96

### 5.4 Potency hierarchy

Potency is position-derived. Points closer to the boundary carry more structural weight.

1. **Corners** (highest): Double-axis purity. Both dimensions at full commitment.
2. **Edge midpoints**: Axial balance points on the boundary.
3. **Other edge points**: Single-axis purity. One dimension at full commitment.
4. **Center points** (lowest): Both dimensions in intermediate states. Compositional, not pure.

## 6. Meaning Hierarchy

Meaning is produced at each structural level of the face, through a distinct mechanism.

### 6.1 Outer lines (the 4 edges of the box)

**Mechanism: Demarcation**

The 4 outer lines define the boundary of the domain. They declare where the domain ends. Without them, interior points are coordinates in unbounded space. The outer lines produce meaning by establishing what belongs to this domain and what does not.

Each outer line holds one axis at its extreme while the other varies freely:
* Left edge (x=0): full dispositional range at minimum constitutive character
* Right edge (x=11): full dispositional range at maximum constitutive character
* Bottom edge (y=0): full constitutive range at minimum dispositional orientation
* Top edge (y=11): full constitutive range at maximum dispositional orientation

The outer lines are the face's **public boundary** — where it faces outward toward other faces. In the Vector Equilibrium, each outer line corresponds to a cuboctahedral edge connecting to a neighboring face.

### 6.2 Inner lines (the grid)

**Mechanism: Graduation**

The inner lines subdivide the enclosed space into distinct positions. Each inner line is a threshold — cross it and you shift one notch along one axis. Without inner lines, the domain exists but is featureless.

The inner lines are the face's **private structure** — the differentiation that exists only within the domain.

### 6.3 Crossings (interior points)

**Mechanism: Composition**

Where a horizontal and vertical coordinate intersect within the grid, both dimensions are partially committed. Neither axis is at its extreme. Meaning arises from the blend ratio — the specific proportion of constitutive character and dispositional orientation at that position.

### 6.4 Corners

**Mechanism: Integration**

Each corner is where two outer lines meet — where a constitutive extreme and a dispositional extreme are simultaneously true. The corner does not blend. It fuses. It identifies two things that everywhere else in the grid are merely in proportion.

The 4 corners carry invariant structural meaning across all faces:

| Corner | Position | Meaning |
|---|---|---|
| (0,0) | Bottom-left | Elemental nature fused with stable tendency |
| (11,0) | Bottom-right | Comprehensive nature fused with stable tendency |
| (0,11) | Top-left | Elemental nature fused with fluid tendency |
| (11,11) | Top-right | Comprehensive nature fused with fluid tendency |

Corners are **joints** — they pin two boundary lines together into a coherent frame.

### 6.5 The face (the plane)

**Mechanism: Enclosure**

The totality of one domain's possibility space. All 144 positions bounded by the 4 outer lines, differentiated by the inner lines, located by crossings, anchored by corners. The face produces meaning by drawing a boundary around everything that can be said within one philosophical domain and giving every possible stance a coordinate.

### 6.6 Summary

```
Outer lines → meaning by demarcation    (boundary established)
Inner lines → meaning by graduation     (interior differentiated)
Crossings   → meaning by composition    (two axes in ratio)
Corners     → meaning by integration    (two axes fused)
Face        → meaning by enclosure      (all ratios bounded)
```

## 8. Spectrums

### 8.1 Definition

A spectrum is a structured opposition between two edge positions on a face. Spectrums represent polarity, tension, variation, contrast, or range across the face.

### 8.2 Derivation

Spectrums arise from the geometry of the grid. Two edge points that are reflections of each other across the grid's center define a spectrum. On a 12×12 grid, position (a,b) on one edge is opposed to position (11-a, 11-b) or its axial reflection.

### 8.3 Counts

The exact spectrum count per face requires formal enumeration under the 12×12 grid geometry. This is a formalization task to be completed during implementation. The count will be larger than the v1 count of 18 per face due to the larger grid.

## 9. Inter-face Architecture (Deductive Cascade)

Everything in this section is **deduced** from the face-level assignments in §5–§7. No element here is independently defined.

### 9.1 The deductive chain

```
Face assignments (§5–§7)          — primary (authored)
  → Nexi                          — deduced from outer lines facing each other
    → Gems                        — deduced from nexi interactions
      → Spokes                    — deduced from gems per face
        → Wheel                   — deduced from all spokes
          → Central gem            — deduced from wheel convergence
```

### 9.2 Nexi

A nexus is a mediating locus between two faces. It exists because two faces' outer lines face each other — their sub-dimensional extremes are structurally related.

* Unique face pairs: C(12,2) = 66
* Directional nexus participations: 12 × 11 = 132
* Each face participates in 11 nexi (one with every other face)

Nexi are stratified by the cube model (§4.4):
* 6 paired nexi (complementary reflection between theoretical/applied counterparts)
* 48 adjacent nexi (proximal concerns on neighboring cube faces)
* 12 opposite nexi (distal concerns across the cube)

Inter-face relationships are determined by **positional correspondence** via the polarity convention, not by point-to-point cross-face edges. Same position across faces = shared archetype (compatible). Opposite position across faces = opposed archetype (tension). This is a property of the shared coordinate system, not an edge in the graph.

### 9.3 Gems

A gem is the condensed integrated state of a nexus interaction — what precipitates when two faces' boundary declarations are held in tension. Each directional nexus participation produces one gem.

* Total gems: 132

### 9.4 Spokes

A spoke is one face's total outward engagement profile — the aggregate of all 11 of its nexi, summarized into a single behavioral signature.

* Total spokes: 12

### 9.5 Wheel

The radial representation of the inter-face system. 12 spokes converging on a central gem.

### 9.6 Central gem

The unique convergence point where all 12 spokes meet. In the Vector Equilibrium geometry, this is the literal geometric center — the point equidistant from all 12 vertices. The central gem is the final deduction: what emerges when every face's outward profile is held together.

## 11. The 48 Corners as Balanced Order

### 11.1 Derivation

All 48 corners are mechanically derived from sub-dimensions. No corner is independently authored. Each corner is the intersection of two sub-dimensional extremes at their respective poles.

### 11.2 Polarity convention

The polarity convention (§7.3) ensures consistent axis orientation across all faces:
* Low (0) = constrained, foundational, conservative, structurally anchored
* High (11) = expansive, synthetic, exploratory, processually open

### 11.3 The four positional groups

The polarity convention produces 4 natural groups of 12 corners each:

| Group | Position | Archetype | Character |
|---|---|---|---|
| Alpha | (0,0) | Foundation | Elemental nature + Stable tendency — the most grounded stance in the domain |
| Beta | (11,0) | Extension | Comprehensive nature + Stable tendency — structurally expansive but temporally grounded |
| Gamma | (0,11) | Transformation | Elemental nature + Fluid tendency — structurally grounded but temporally expansive |
| Delta | (11,11) | Integration | Comprehensive nature + Fluid tendency — the most open stance in the domain |

### 11.4 Balance

Balance is not authored. It emerges from three design commitments:

1. Each face's sub-dimensions are chosen so that low = constrained and high = expansive (polarity convention)
2. Corners are mechanically derived as intersections (no independent authoring)
3. No sub-dimensional extreme label is reused across faces (uniqueness)

These three commitments ensure that:
* All 48 corner labels are distinct
* All Alpha corners share the character of "maximally grounded" across 12 different philosophical domains
* All Delta corners share the character of "maximally open" across 12 different philosophical domains
* Same-group corners across faces have natural compatibility
* Opposite-group corners (Alpha↔Delta, Beta↔Gamma) carry natural tension

## 12. Harmonization

### 12.1 Definition

Harmonization is the property by which paired faces are structurally tuned to receive each other's activations. It is not a feature that is turned on or an algorithm that runs. It is a consequence of structural integrity at the root.

### 12.2 Basis

Harmonization requires two conditions:

1. **Commensurability** — the polarity convention ensures all faces speak the same structural language. Same position = same archetype on every face.
2. **Directionality** — the cube pairing determines which specific face is tuned to which. Not every face harmonizes equally with every other. Paired faces (inside/outside of the same cube face) are maximally tuned.

### 12.3 Mechanism

When the engine processes a prompt and activates positions on one face, the paired face receives those activations through positional correspondence. Position (3,8) on Ontology activates the theoretical understanding of "moderately particular, strongly dynamic being." Position (3,8) on Praxeology — Ontology's paired counterpart — is "moderately individual, strongly proactive action." The pairing says: this understanding and this action belong to each other.

The correspondence is not forced. It is a consequence of the shared surface and aligned coordinates. The math computes on each face independently. But because the faces are paired and aligned, the outputs naturally resonate.

### 12.4 Emergent beauty

The theory of this Construct: build the geometry right, align the faces honestly, let the math run without forcing the outcome — and the emergent order will have aesthetic coherence because the foundations are sound. The beauty is not authored into the output. It is a consequence of structural integrity at the root, computed faithfully through the deductive cascade.

| Degree vocabulary | The qualitative mapping from grid positions to named intensity levels (strongly, moderately, balanced) |
| PRECEDES chain | The causal ordering of 12 domains connected by 11 directed edges |
| Deductive cascade | The derivation sequence from face-level assignments through nexi, gems, spokes, wheel to central gem |
| Adjacent nexus | A nexus between domains whose cube faces share an edge (48 total) |
| Opposite nexus | A nexus between domains on cube faces across the cube (12 total) |
| Crossing | An interior point where two grid lines intersect — produces meaning by composition |
| Construction question | A parameterized question template at a specific grid position, instantiated per domain |
| Replacement string | The domain-specific noun phrase substituted for {domain} in construction questions |

## 14. Inference Machinery

### 14.1 Overview

The Construct produces meaning at every point through inference, not individual authoring. Three authored layers combine to determine the meaning at any coordinate on any face.

### 14.2 The three layers

**Layer 1: Axis meta-meaning** — X = constitutive character (what kind of thing). Y = dispositional orientation (how it engages). This holds on every face.

**Layer 2: Polarity convention** — Low (0) = constrained, foundational, elemental. High (11) = expansive, synthetic, exploratory. This holds on every axis of every face.

**Layer 3: Sub-dimensions** — Each face names what constitutive character and dispositional orientation mean in its domain (§7.3).

### 14.3 Inference rules by position type

| Position type | What's fixed | What varies | Inference method |
|---|---|---|---|
| Corner | Both axes at extreme | Nothing | Fusion — two extremes collapse into a single committed stance |
| Edge midpoint | One axis at extreme, other at balance | Nothing | Axial balance at the boundary |
| Other edge | One axis at extreme | The other axis | One extreme held, other at specified degree |
| Interior | Nothing | Both axes | Composition — two axes blended at specified degrees |

### 14.4 Degree vocabulary

Positions along each axis map to qualitative degrees:

| Position | Degree |
|---|---|
| 0 | fully [low_label] |
| 1–2 | strongly [low_label] |
| 3–4 | moderately [low_label] |
| 5–6 | balanced between [low_label] and [high_label] |
| 7–8 | moderately [high_label] |
| 9–10 | strongly [high_label] |
| 11 | fully [high_label] |

### 14.5 Question generation

Construction questions are generated from 144 authored templates, one per grid position. Each template is parameterized with `{domain}` and produces 12 domain-specific questions. The templates are authored by reading the geometric meaning at each position (using the three inference layers) and phrasing that meaning as a question useful for prompt construction.

Total: 144 templates × 12 domains = 1728 construction questions.

The templates are organized by zone for tractable authoring:

| Zone | Count | Character |
|---|---|---|
| Corners | 4 | Fusion — most committed stances |
| Edge midpoints | 8 | Balance at the boundary |
| Other edge | 32 | One axis at extreme, other varying |
| Near-edge interior | 36 | Transition from pure to blended |
| Deep interior | 64 | Full composition of both axes |

### 14.6 Nexus meaning generation

Each nexus meaning is generated from the relationship between two faces' sub-dimensions. The generation asks: how does face A's constitutive axis relate to face B's constitutive axis, and how does face A's dispositional axis relate to face B's dispositional axis?

Paired nexi (§4.4) carry complementary meaning — theoretical ↔ practical reflection. Adjacent and opposite nexi carry varying degrees of affinity and tension determined by sub-dimensional proximity.
